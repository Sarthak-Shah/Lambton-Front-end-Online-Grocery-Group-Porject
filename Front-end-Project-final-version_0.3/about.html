  1. In 1994, the first grocery store in Ottawa Queensway opened its doors to the public. The weather outside was bitterly cold, with temperatures hovering around freezing and a gusty wind that made the air feel even colder. Despite the adverse conditions, the store was filled with excitement as shoppers rushed to explore the aisles and stock up on their favorite items.

  2. The store itself was a small, family-owned operation, with a limited selection of goods but a friendly and welcoming atmosphere. The owners(Wilson Family) had spent months preparing for the grand opening, stocking the shelves with fresh produce, and other essential items.They had also hired a team of friendly and knowledgeable staff members who were eager to help customers find what they were looking for.
  3. As the day went on, more and more people braved the cold weather to visit the store. Some came to do their weekly shopping, while others were simply curious to see what the new store had to offer. Despite the cold and draught, the atmosphere inside the store was warm and inviting, with customers chatting with one another and the staff members as they shopped.

  4. As the first day of business came to a close, the store owners were pleased with the turnout and the positive response from their customers. They knew that running a grocery store in such a challenging climate would not be easy, but they were determined to make it a success. And as the years went by, their hard work and dedication paid off, as the store grew and flourished, becoming a staple of the Ottawa Queensway community.
